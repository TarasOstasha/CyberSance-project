Data Collection

It is our tradition to go above and beyond - to fulfil the unique needs of our clients. There are two main factors in our work that enable us to achieve that:

  • Custom Solutions

    • Our engineers continuously build tailored scrapers or bots with powerful new generation tools.

  • AI-Powered Analysis

    • We use machine learning to analyze data trends, detect anomalies, and generate insights.

  • Deep Web

    • Refers to areas of the internet not indexed by search engines.
    • Includes databases, academic archives, and private forums.
    • Useful for gathering industry-specific research, threat intelligence, or monitoring private data leaks.

  • Dark Web

    • Encrypted and anonymized parts of the internet, accessible through specialized tools.
    • Often used for illegal activities, making it a hub for cybercrime intelligence (e.g., stolen data, hacking tools, illicit transactions).

  • Chatrooms

    • Includes platforms like Discord, IRC, and forums used for specific niche discussions.
    • Can provide real-time monitoring of discussions related to hacking, activism, or emerging threats.

  • Messaging and Calling Apps

    • Includes exclusive WhatsApp and Signal collections, among typical others.
    • Can deliver valuable information for government agencies, especially in the context of physical security. Will be useful to private companies as well, while strategic planning amidst global developments, activism, hacktivism and similar events.

  • Social Media

    • Platforms like Twitter, Reddit, and LinkedIn for open-source intelligence (OSINT) and beyond.
    • Useful for trend analysis, public sentiment, and identifying phishing attempts or scams; fraud and threat activities might also be detected.

  • Cyber Threat Intelligence

    • Monitoring for breaches, compromised credentials, and discussions of exploits.
    • Identifying individuals or groups planning attacks or discussing vulnerabilities.

  • Brand Protection

    • Detecting counterfeit goods, leaked intellectual property, or slanderous posts.
    • Responding to threats to organizational reputation.

  • Market Research

    • Understanding consumer sentiment or industry trends through forums and social media.
    • Identifying emerging markets or unmet needs based on discussions in niche communities.

  • Law Enforcement

    • Evidence gathering for legal actions against cybercriminals.
    • Investigating criminal activity or coordinating with authorities for threat mitigation.
